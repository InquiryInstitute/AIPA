[appendix]
== Reading List
:leveloffset: 1

[[reading-list]]
Readings are organized by chapter. Each chapter's lectures reference this list; see <<reading-ch01>> through <<reading-ch12>> for direct links to a chapter's readings. Full bibliographic details are in the Bibliography.

=== Chapter 1: Intelligence as Process
[[reading-ch01]]

* Russell & Norvig, _AIMA_ <<RussellNorvig2020>> — Comprehensive treatment of agents, search, and classical AI.
* Latour, _Reassembling the Social_ <<Latour2005>> — Actor-Network Theory; useful for understanding AI as infrastructure.
* Foucault, _Discipline and Punish_ <<Foucault1975>> — Power, norms, and institutional context.

=== Chapter 2: AI in Practice
[[reading-ch02]]

* NIST, _AI Risk Management Framework_ <<NIST2023>> — Governance and risk for AI systems.
* EU AI Act <<EU2024>> — Regulatory framework for high-risk AI.
* Foucault, _Power/Knowledge_ <<Foucault1980>> — Governance as constitutive.

=== Chapter 3: Search and Planning
[[reading-ch03]]

* Russell & Norvig, _AIMA_ <<RussellNorvig2020>> — Search, planning, heuristics, A*.
* Silver et al., "Mastering the Game of Go" <<Silver2016>> — Search + neural networks.
* Sutton & Barto, _Reinforcement Learning_ <<Sutton2018>> — MDPs, value iteration.

=== Chapter 4: Learning from Data
[[reading-ch04]]

* Box, "Science and Statistics" <<Box1976>> — All models are wrong; some useful.
* Geman et al., "Bias/Variance Dilemma" <<Geman1992>> — Decomposition of generalization error.
* Vapnik, _Statistical Learning Theory_ <<Vapnik1995>> — VC dimension, generalization bounds.

=== Chapter 5: Neural Systems and Representation
[[reading-ch05]]

* LeCun, Bengio & Hinton, "Deep Learning" <<LeCun2015>> — Survey of deep learning.
* Rumelhart et al., "Back-Propagating Errors" <<Rumelhart1986>> — Backprop algorithm.
* Krizhevsky et al., "ImageNet" <<Krizhevsky2012>> — CNNs for vision.
* Rosenblatt, "Perceptron" <<Rosenblatt1958>> — Original perceptron.

=== Chapter 6: Language and Models
[[reading-ch06]]

* Vaswani et al., "Attention Is All You Need" <<Vaswani2017>> — Transformer architecture.
* Brown et al., "Few-Shot Learners" <<Brown2020>> — GPT-3, in-context learning.
* Wei et al., "Chain-of-Thought" <<Wei2022>> — CoT prompting.
* Mikolov et al., "Word Representations" <<Mikolov2013>> — Word2vec, embeddings.

=== Chapter 7: Memory Systems
[[reading-ch07]]

* Lewis et al., "RAG" <<Lewis2020>> — Retrieval-augmented generation.
* Izacard & Grave, "DPR" <<Izacard2020>> — Dense passage retrieval.
* Bartlett, _Remembering_ <<Bartlett1932>> — Memory as reconstruction.
* Reimers & Gurevych, "Sentence-BERT" <<Reimers2019>> — Sentence embeddings.

=== Chapter 8: Reasoning and Inference
[[reading-ch08]]

* Pearl, _Probabilistic Reasoning_ <<Pearl1988>> — Bayesian networks, inference.
* Wei et al., "Chain-of-Thought" <<Wei2022>> — CoT reasoning.
* Russell & Norvig, _AIMA_ <<RussellNorvig2020>> — Logic, resolution, planning.

=== Chapter 9: Acting in the World
[[reading-ch09]]

* Yao et al., "ReAct" <<Yao2022>> — Reasoning and acting interleaved.
* Schick et al., "Toolformer" <<Schick2023>> — LLMs learning to use tools.
* Russell & Norvig, _AIMA_ <<RussellNorvig2020>> — Agent architectures.

=== Chapter 10: Architectures of Intelligence
[[reading-ch10]]

* Fielding, "REST" <<Fielding2000>> — Architectural styles for network-based software.
* Schick et al., "Toolformer" <<Schick2023>> — Tool use in LLMs.
* Yao et al., "ReAct" <<Yao2022>> — Multi-step tool use.

=== Chapter 11: AI in Institutions
[[reading-ch11]]

* EU AI Act <<EU2024>> — Regulatory framework.
* NIST AI RMF <<NIST2023>> — Risk management.
* Buolamwini & Gebru, "Gender Shades" <<Buolamwini2018>> — Bias in classification.
* Mehrabi et al., "Bias and Fairness in ML" <<Mehrabi2019>> — Survey.

=== Chapter 12: The Future of Intelligence
[[reading-ch12]]

* Norman, _Design of Everyday Things_ <<Norman2013>> — Interface design.
* NIST AI RMF <<NIST2023>> — Evaluation and risk.
* Dewey, _Democracy and Education_ <<Dewey1916>> — Problem definition, stakeholders.

=== Postmodern fiction and film
[[reading-fiction-film]]

Optional readings and viewings that extend the postmodern lens (see DESIGN §9.1). Suggested for discussion prompts and thematic depth.

*Fiction*

* Borges, "The Garden of Forking Paths" <<Borges1941>> — Branching time, labyrinth as state space. Suggested for Ch3 (Search).
* Dick, _Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep?_ <<Dick1968>> — Reality, replication, memory. Suggested for Ch11 (Governance) and Ch7 (Memory).
* Gibson, _Neuromancer_ <<Gibson1984>> — Distributed agency, networks. Suggested for Ch9–Ch10 (Agents, orchestration).
* Pynchon, _Gravity's Rainbow_ <<Pynchon1973>> — Unreliable narrative, paranoia, no single story; "wrong questions" and problem framing. Suggested for Ch12 (Problem definition).

*Film*

* _Blade Runner_ <<BladeRunner1982>> — Memory, identity, replicants. Suggested for Ch11 (Governance).
* _Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind_ <<EternalSunshine2004>> — Memory erasure and reconstruction. Suggested for Ch7 (Memory).
* _The Matrix_ <<Matrix1999>> — Simulation, representation. Suggested for Ch4/Ch6 (representation) or Ch11.
* _Her_ <<Her2013>> — AI as participant, agency, mediation. Suggested for Ch9–Ch10.
* _Memento_ <<Memento2000>> — Memory as reconstruction, unreliability. Suggested for Ch7 (Memory).
* _Ex Machina_ <<ExMachina2014>> — Agency, observation, governance. Suggested for Ch11.
